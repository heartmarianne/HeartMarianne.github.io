History of CPSU

CPSU started as Negros Occidental Agricultural School (NONAS) and was dubbed as the first Agricultural Institution in the country established by a Filipino Superintendent named Jose P. Crisanto immediately after World War II in 1946. The Institution was converted to Negros Occidental Agricultural College (NOAC) by the virtue of Presidential Authority on September 6, 1977. NOAC then was converted into state college known as Negros State College of Agriculture (NSCA) by virtue of R.A 9141 date july 3, 2001.[3] By virtue of Republic Act (R.A.) 10228,NSCA was converted to Central Philippines State University in 2012.

Today, CPSU Main Campus is traversed by an all-weather national highway and accessible to regular buses and jeepneys plying to Bacolod via Kabankalan City in Negros Occidental side in Dumaguete City via the Municipality of Mabinay in the Oriental side.
